Meridian Support

Help with the Meridian habit tracker for iOS.

Report a bug or ask a question

Open a GitHub Issue — include iOS version, Meridian version (Settings → About), and steps to reproduce. Avoid posting habit names or other personal content in public issues when possible.

Accounts and cloud backup

Accounts are optional. You can use Meridian without signing in; habits stay on your device only.

  • Sign in — Settings → Account → Sign in with Apple or Sign in with Google. Apple and Google buttons have equal prominence.
  • What syncs — habits, completion history, and selected settings (week start, haptics). Widgets always read on-device data; the app syncs to the cloud in the background.
  • First sign-in — if you already have local habits, Meridian asks whether to upload them to your account. You can decline and keep cloud empty until you add new data.
  • New phone — install Meridian, sign in with the same Apple or Google account, and wait for sync to finish (requires network). Habits should appear after the first successful pull.
  • Offline — logging and widgets still work offline. Changes save locally and sync when you are back online.
  • Sign out — Settings → Account → Sign out. Local habits remain on the device; cloud session is cleared.
  • Delete account — Settings → Account → Delete account (two-step confirmation). Removes cloud backup and your account. You can choose to keep or delete local habits on the device.

Sync troubleshooting

  • “Offline — changes saved locally” — expected without network. Sync resumes when connectivity returns.
  • “Session expired” — sign in again with the same provider (Apple or Google).
  • Data mismatch across devices — open Meridian on each device while online and leave the app in the foreground briefly. Last update wins for each habit.
  • Widget out of date after sync — open the app once; widgets refresh from the on-device snapshot after sync merges.

Quick answers

  • Widgets show no habits? Open Meridian and add at least one habit, then add the widget from the iOS Home Screen or Lock Screen widget gallery.
  • Widget tap doesn’t update? Ensure the widget is a Meridian interactive widget (tap the metaphor to log a rep). Reload widgets after large data changes.
  • Weekly habits — week boundaries follow Settings → Week starts on (locale, Sunday, or Monday).
  • Data without an account — habits stay on your device only. See our Privacy Policy.
  • Data with an account — synced habits are stored on Supabase-hosted infrastructure. See Privacy Policy → Accounts and cloud sync.
  • After an app update — if habits disappear locally, Meridian may have moved your old database to a StoreBackups/ folder on device. If you use an account, sign in and sync after checking local recovery. Contact us via GitHub Issues if you need help.
