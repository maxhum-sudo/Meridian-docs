Meridian Privacy Policy

Effective date: 18 June 2026 · App: Meridian (com.maximillianhum.Meridian)

Meridian is a habit tracker for iPhone and iPad, developed by Maximillian Hum. This Privacy Policy describes how the app handles information. Meridian is local-first: you can use it without an account and your habits stay on your device. If you choose to sign in, optional cloud backup and sync use our infrastructure provider, Supabase. Use of Meridian is also governed by our Terms of Service.

Summary

  • No account required. Sign-in is optional. Without an account, we do not receive your habit data.
  • Optional cloud backup. If you sign in with Apple or Google, habit data you choose to sync is stored on Supabase-hosted servers so you can restore across devices.
  • No analytics or advertising SDKs. Meridian does not use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking frameworks.
  • No sale of data. We do not sell or share your information with third parties for marketing.

Information stored on your device

Whether or not you use an account, habits, progress, streaks, and settings are stored locally using Apple’s SwiftData and on-device storage. This includes:

  • Habit names, targets, cadence (daily or weekly), and completion history
  • Display preferences (for example haptics and week-start day)
  • Tutorial and onboarding state
  • Sign-in session tokens in the device Keychain, if you are signed in

If you are not signed in, habit data is not transmitted off your device as part of normal app use.

Accounts and cloud sync (optional)

If you create an account through Sign in with Apple or Sign in with Google, Meridian can back up and sync selected data to cloud storage operated by Supabase on our behalf.

What we store when you sign in

  • Account identifiers — a user ID issued by our backend, and the provider identifier from Apple or Google (used only to authenticate you and link your data to your account)
  • Email address (optional) — if your sign-in provider shares one (Apple may provide a private relay address via Hide My Email)
  • Habit data — habit names, targets, metaphors, cadence, sort order, completion counts, per-day history, streak-related fields, and related sync metadata
  • Synced settings — display preferences that affect habit periods (for example week-start day and haptics), if you have cloud sync enabled

We do not upload your contacts, photos, location, health data, advertising identifier, or device-wide analytics through Meridian.

How sync works

  • The app reads and writes habits on your device first. Cloud sync runs in the background when you are signed in and online.
  • Data is sent to our backend over TLS (encrypted in transit).
  • Data at rest is stored in a Supabase-managed PostgreSQL database. Supabase encrypts data at rest using industry-standard protections on their infrastructure.
  • Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets do not connect to the network. They read on-device snapshots only; the main app handles any cloud sync.

Where cloud data is processed

Cloud backup data is stored in the region configured for our Supabase project (currently East US 2 (Ohio), United States). If you are outside the United States, your synced habit data may be processed and stored in that region. Support messages you send through GitHub may be processed wherever those services operate.

Retention and deletion

  • Synced habit data is kept while your account is active and you use cloud backup.
  • You can sign out at any time in Settings → Account. Signing out stops sync and removes session tokens from your device; local habits remain on the device.
  • You can delete your account in Settings → Account. This deletes your cloud backup and account identifiers from our systems. Deletion is processed promptly; residual copies may persist in encrypted backups for up to 30 days before purge.
  • When you delete your account, you may choose to keep habits on the device or remove local data as part of the same flow.

Service provider (subprocessor)

We use Supabase, Inc. to host authentication and cloud database services. Supabase processes account and habit data only as needed to provide backup and sync. Their privacy practices are described at supabase.com/privacy.

Sign-in is also subject to Apple’s privacy policy (Sign in with Apple) and Google’s privacy policy (Sign in with Google), as applicable.

Widgets and App Group

Meridian includes Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets. To show and update habit progress, the main app and widget extension share a snapshot of habit data through an Apple App Group (group.com.maximillianhum.Meridian) stored in on-device shared preferences. Widget data stays on your device and is not sent directly to Supabase or any other third party.

Store recovery backups

If Meridian cannot open your saved data after an app update, it may move the previous database files into a StoreBackups/ folder on your device before creating a fresh store. These backups remain on your device only and are not uploaded to cloud backup unless you later sign in and sync.

Diagnostics and logging

Meridian may write diagnostic messages to the on-device unified log (Apple’s os.Logger) for debugging—for example when a save fails, sync fails, or the data store needs recovery. These logs stay on your device unless you choose to share them (for example by exporting a sysdiagnose or describing an issue on GitHub). We do not automatically receive log data.

Our Supabase project may retain short-lived server access logs (for example request metadata) for security and operations. Those logs are not intended to contain habit names or completion details.

Apple and distribution

Meridian is distributed through the Apple App Store and may be offered via TestFlight during beta. Apple may collect information related to downloads, purchases, crash reports you opt into, and device identifiers according to Apple’s privacy policies. That processing is between you and Apple, not Meridian’s developer.

Support channels

If you contact us through Meridian Support or GitHub Issues, any information you choose to include (for example iOS version, app version, or steps to reproduce a bug) is handled under those platforms’ policies. Only share what you are comfortable making public on GitHub. Do not post habit names or other sensitive content in public issues unless necessary.

What we do not collect

Meridian does not access your contacts, photos, camera, microphone, location, health data, or advertising identifier. We do not use Meridian to build advertising profiles or sell personal information.

Children

Meridian is suitable for general audiences and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us through an account or support channel, contact us and we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

International users

If you use an account, synced habit data may be stored in East US 2 (Ohio), United States through Supabase. If you use support channels hosted outside your country, information you submit there may be processed according to those services’ rules. You may use Meridian without an account to keep habit history on your device only.

Changes

We may update this policy when the app changes (for example when account features are added or our service providers change). The effective date at the top will be revised. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

Questions about privacy: Meridian Support or GitHub Issues.
